What is an Off-Grid Solar System?

An off-grid solar system is a standalone power setup that operates completely independently from the traditional electric grid. Unlike on-grid systems, it uses batteries to store energy generated during sunshine hours for use at night or during cloudy periods, ensuring 24/7 power availability.

landscape

When to Use Off-Grid Solar?

  • check_circle You live in a remote area with no utility grid connection
  • check_circle Your area faces extreme and frequent power cuts
  • check_circle The grid is unreliable or too expensive to connect
  • check_circle Farmhouses, cabins, or rural industrial setups
info

Important to Know

Not for Most Urban Homes: If you have reliable grid connection, on-grid solar is more cost-effective, requires less maintenance, and offers government subsidies.

No Government Subsidy: Off-grid systems are not eligible for subsidies under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana.

Higher Initial Cost: Due to battery requirements, off-grid systems cost more upfront than on-grid alternatives.

On-Grid vs Off-Grid: Which is Better?

For most urban homes with reliable grid access, on-grid solar is the better choice. Here's why:

payments

Lower Cost

On-grid systems cost significantly less as they don't require expensive battery banks.

Example: 3kW on-grid ₹1.02L-₹1.57L (after subsidy) vs 3kW off-grid ₹2.55L-₹3L

Government Subsidy

Eligible for ₹30,000-₹78,000 subsidy under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana.

Off-grid systems don't qualify for any government subsidies.

Less Maintenance

No batteries means minimal maintenance - just occasional panel cleaning.

Off-grid requires battery maintenance and replacement every 5-7 years.

Faster ROI

On-grid systems typically break even in 3-4 years vs 6-7 years for off-grid.

Lower upfront cost and subsidies accelerate return on investment.

Earn Credits

Excess power fed to grid earns you credits, reducing future electricity bills.

Off-grid can only store excess in batteries (limited capacity).

Better for Environment

No battery manufacturing/disposal means smaller environmental footprint.

Lead-acid batteries require careful disposal and recycling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about off-grid solar systems

How much does an off-grid solar system cost?

Off-grid solar system costs vary based on capacity, panel type, inverter quality, and battery type:

  • • 2 kW: ₹1,70,000 - ₹2,00,000
  • • 3 kW: ₹2,55,000 - ₹3,00,000
  • • 4 kW: ₹3,40,000 - ₹4,00,000
  • • 5 kW: ₹4,25,000 - ₹5,00,000
What is the disadvantage of off-grid solar?

The main disadvantage is longer ROI (6-7 years vs 3-4 years for on-grid) due to higher upfront costs from batteries. Other considerations include battery replacement every 5-7 years, higher maintenance, and no government subsidies available.

Is there a subsidy for off-grid solar systems?

No. The Indian government currently offers subsidies only for on-grid rooftop solar systems under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, not for off-grid systems.

How many batteries are required for off-grid solar systems?

Battery requirements depend on system size and your backup needs:

  • • 3 kW system: 8-9 batteries of 100 AH capacity
  • • 5 kW system: 10-11 batteries of 500 AH capacity

Lithium-ion batteries require fewer units than lead-acid but cost more.

How long do off-grid solar batteries last?

Lead-acid batteries typically last 5-7 years with proper maintenance. Lithium-ion batteries can last 10-15 years but cost significantly more upfront. Battery lifespan depends on depth of discharge, charging patterns, and maintenance.

Can I add batteries to my existing on-grid system?

Adding batteries to an on-grid system converts it to a hybrid system (not off-grid). This gives you backup power during outages while still maintaining grid connection. A hybrid inverter and battery bank are required for this upgrade.

What size off-grid system do I need for my home?

System size depends on your daily energy consumption and backup requirements. Calculate your total wattage of appliances and hours of use. A 3kW system suits small homes (2-3 BHK) with basic appliances. 5kW+ needed for larger homes with AC, water heaters, etc.

Do off-grid systems work during cloudy days?

Yes, off-grid systems work during cloudy days by drawing power from the battery bank charged during sunny periods. However, prolonged cloudy weather can deplete batteries. Many off-grid setups include a backup generator for extended low-sun periods.
