What is an On-Grid Solar System?

An on-grid or grid-tied solar system is directly connected to your local electricity grid. It allows you to consume solar power during the day and export excess energy back to the grid for credits.

How On-Grid Solar Systems Work

A seamless 6-step process that powers your home while earning you credits from excess generation.

Step 1

Solar Capture

Solar panels absorb sunlight and generate DC electricity throughout the day.

Step 2

DC to AC Conversion

Inverter converts DC electricity to AC power usable by home appliances.

Step 3

Power Your Home

Solar AC power is used first to run all your appliances and lights.

Step 4

Export Surplus

Excess energy is automatically sent to the grid via net metering.

Step 5

Earn Credits

Bi-directional meter records exported units as credits on your bill.

Step 6

Use Credits at Night

At night, draw grid power and offset it with your accumulated credits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about on-grid solar systems

How much can I save with an on-grid solar system? expand_more

You can save up to 90% on your electricity bills. A typical 3kW system can save approximately ₹30,000 per year, resulting in total savings of ₹16.58 lakhs over 25 years.

What is the payback period for on-grid solar? expand_more

With government subsidies, the payback period is typically 3-5 years. After that, you enjoy 20+ years of free electricity.

Will I have power during grid outages? expand_more

No, on-grid systems automatically shut down during power cuts due to anti-islanding protection for grid safety. If backup power is essential, consider a hybrid solar system with battery storage.

How does net metering work? expand_more

Net metering allows you to export excess solar energy to the grid. A bi-directional meter tracks both import and export. Exported units are credited to your bill, which you can use later (typically at night).

How much rooftop space do I need? expand_more

Approximately 100 sq ft of shadow-free rooftop space is required per kW. A 3kW system needs around 300 sq ft, and a 5kW system needs 500 sq ft.

What maintenance is required? expand_more

On-grid systems require minimal maintenance. Clean panels 2-3 times per year and check inverter performance monthly. SolarForce includes 45 free maintenance visits over 5 years.

How long does installation take? expand_more

Physical installation typically takes just 1-2 days (approximately 8 hours). The entire process including permissions and net metering approval takes 2-4 weeks depending on your local DISCOM.

Do I get a warranty? expand_more

Yes! Solar panels come with 25-year performance warranty, inverter has 5-10 year warranty, and mounting structure has lifetime warranty. SolarForce also provides comprehensive service warranty.
